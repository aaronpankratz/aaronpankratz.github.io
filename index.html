Hi! I'm writing this so that you can learn more about me and what it's like to work with me. Think of this as a README for my professional self.

About me

First and formost I'm an engineer. I love to read/write code, work with data, and build things! I'm also a software and systems architect, technical lead, mediator between engineers and stakeholders, part time manager, and engineering mentor.

Experience

I started learning about computers from an early age. MS-DOS, Mario teaches typing, and the Lemmings video game were my earliest exposures to computing. My grandfather was an engineer and taught me some basics of HTML. My father was a software engineer for 31+ years. He worked on programming hard real-time operating systems and data processing systems for mission critical applications and taught me computers, typing, the internet, and programming.

I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin with my B.S. in Computer Science and since then I've been working on full-stack web applications. I've worked as an iOS mobile developer. I've built an mobile app for a startup from scratch using React Native while also working full-stack. Since 2020 I've been working on building SOA, scalable micro service systems and focusing on back end technologies.

I've worked at a variety of companies, including startups from 5-50 people, mid-size companies from 100-5000 people, and a large tech company with 50,000+ people. I've learned many lessons and patterns from all the different people and technologies I've worked with. I enjoy the challenge of mentoring. I've mentored more than a dozen people from all experience levels, high school students and software apprentices to senior engineers.

Working style

I've been working remotely since 2018.

Early mornings for me are for focused work. I block off the first 2-3 hours of my day to focus on writing/reviewing code, technical specs, scoping projects and executing high priority tasks. I ignore all communication channels and distractions during this time so that I can maximize my flow state and maintain peak productivity levels. If you don't get a response from me during this time, please be patient, I promise I'll get back to you.

Late morning through the rest of the day, I'm responding to chat, email and meeting with engineers, stakeholders, and managers to collaborate. If I have any gaps without meetings I work on various low priority tasks.

Personality

My DISC Type is C, analyst. I tend to be an objective thinker and prioritize accuracy and results. I pay attention to small details and take a systematic aproach to solving problems. I naturally enjoy experimenting with many solutions to a problem. I get energy from order and organization. Unreliable, unproven information drains me.

At work my personality shifts toward SC, stabilizer. I thrive when establishing consistent daily routines, creating stability, predictability, and trust. I value careful planning and preparation.

My top five Gallup Clifton strengths are:

  • Learner: Learning new skills and information is one of my favorite things to do.

  • Achiever: I'm highly motivated by acheiving a goal which plays into my results oriented mindset.

  • Analytical: I have an analytical mindset and I typically want to understand all the options and information before making a decision.

  • Intellection: I enjoy discussing big ideas, puzzles, problems, and strategies.

  • Self-Assurance: I don't get too anxious about things and tend to stay optimistic, assuming everything will work out. I don't mind being the first to be vulnerable in a group to help others open up.
